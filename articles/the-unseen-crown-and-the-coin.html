The Unseen Crown and the Quiet Wisdom of a Coin

A reflection on responsibility, uncertainty, and the moments between overthinking and choice.
12 July 2023 · 15 mins read · Prajwal K.

The Weight We Don’t Always Talk About

There is a kind of weight that doesn’t announce itself. It doesn’t feel dramatic or cinematic most of the time—it just sits quietly in the background of thought. That’s what people mean when they say “heavy is the head that wears the crown.”

But the crown is not always about authority or status. More often, it appears in ordinary life: choosing a career, deciding whether to stay or leave, or even wondering what happens if we choose wrong.

The strange part is that the more we care, the heavier it feels. Not because the decision is objectively large, but because the mind keeps expanding its importance into multiple possible futures.

When Thinking Starts to Loop

At some point, thinking stops helping and starts repeating itself. We revisit the same arguments in slightly different forms, hoping one of them will finally feel decisive.

It is not confusion in the usual sense. It is excess awareness—seeing too many angles at once, and losing the ability to settle into one.

And slowly, the pressure to make the “right” choice becomes the fear of making any choice at all.

The Strange Honesty of a Coin Flip

This is where something simple enters: a coin.

On the surface, it looks like surrendering decision-making to chance. But its real function is not to decide—it is to reveal.

The real moment is not when it lands, but when it is still in the air. In that brief suspension, the mind reacts before logic can interfere.

A preference appears. A subtle pull. A quiet resistance. And suddenly, what was hidden under overthinking becomes visible.

The coin doesn’t replace thought. It interrupts it just long enough for truth to surface.

Crown and Coin: Two Ways of Being Human

The crown represents responsibility—the demand to think, care, and consider consequences.

The coin represents limitation—the acceptance that certainty is often unavailable.

Too much crown leads to heaviness. Too much coin leads to drift. Most lives move constantly between the two.

Making Peace With Imperfect Decisions

Most decisions are not made with full clarity. They are made with enough clarity to move forward.

And often, the relief after deciding does not come from knowing it was correct—but from the silence that follows the end of internal conflict.

That silence is underrated. It is what overthinking quietly takes away.

A Small Kind of Wisdom

Wisdom is not always about choosing correctly. It is about recognizing when thought has done its job and when it has started repeating itself.

The crown reminds us that choices matter. The coin reminds us that uncertainty is normal.

And between them lies a simple truth: we will rarely feel fully ready, but we will still have to choose anyway.
